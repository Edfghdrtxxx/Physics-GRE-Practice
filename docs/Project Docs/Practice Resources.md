
Here's the vetted list, most conservative first. Everything below was verified by me today via search/fetch; sources linked.

**Tier 1 — Official (ETS, the exam maker). Trust fully.**

1. [ETS Physics practice book (GR1777)](https://www.ets.org/pdfs/gre/practice-book-physics.pdf) — a real, full-length released exam with answer key, free from ETS directly. Also listed on the [ETS Subject Test practice books page](https://www.ets.org/gre/test-takers/subject-tests/prepare/practice-books.html).
2. Older released real exams — GR0877, GR9677, GR9277, GR8677. ETS no longer hosts these, but university physics departments mirror them, e.g. Harvey Mudd: [GR0877](https://media.physics.hmc.edu/media/docs/gr0877.pdf), [GR1777](https://media.physics.hmc.edu/media/docs/gre1777.pdf); Minnesota SPS: [GR0877](http://sps.physics.umn.edu/exam_GR0877.pdf). The questions themselves are genuine ETS exams — this is the core of any serious prep.

**Tier 2 — Widely used in physics departments. Trust for solutions/strategy, with the caveat that they're not official.**

3. [GREPhysics.NET](http://www.grephysics.net/ans/all-solutions_list.php) — complete worked solutions to all released exams (GR8677/9277/9677/0177), by Yosun Chang. Solutions have user comments that catch errors, but they're community-checked, not ETS-checked.
4. *Conquering the Physics GRE* (Kahn & Anderson, [Cambridge University Press, 3rd ed.](https://www.cambridge.org/9781108409568)) — the standard prep book, written by MIT physics grad students, recommended by departments like [Ohio State](https://physics.osu.edu/physics_gre). Buy it (~$30 on [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Conquering-Physics-GRE-Yoni-Kahn/dp/1108409563)); avoid pirated PDF copies floating around.
5. [physicsgre.com forums](https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=109) — long-running community with solution threads (including [GR1777 solutions](https://physicsgre.com/viewtopic.php?t=127211)) and score/admissions data points. Treat individual posts as unverified.

**What I'd leave out:** third-party question banks (Career Employer, Quizlet, etc.) — not real ETS questions, no named physicist review. Given your "conservative is better" rule, five released real exams (~500 questions) plus the Kahn & Anderson book is already more material than most high scorers use.

Two things to note, separated per your preference: the copyright point (ETS owns the exams — keep PDFs private, link don't republish) is fact; my ranking of tiers is judgment based on what physics departments recommend, not on any official certification of these sites.
